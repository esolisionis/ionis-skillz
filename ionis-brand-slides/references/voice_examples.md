# Voice examples — off-brand vs. on-brand

Concrete sentence-level calibration. The brand voice attributes (driven, inquisitive, pioneering, confident, passionate) are abstract; these paired examples translate them into actual copy decisions.

Load this file when drafting slide headlines, body copy, opener narratives, CTAs, or when a user asks you to "make this sound more Ionis."

## Headlines

| ❌ Off-brand | ✅ On-brand | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "Overview of Our Pipeline" | "Late-stage and accelerating" | Declarative, not labelled. Cuts the "overview of" cliché. |
| "Introduction to RNA-Targeted Medicines" | "We pioneered RNA-targeted medicines." | Claims heritage instead of teaching it. Pioneering attribute. |
| "Our Approach to Innovation" | "Push beyond the impossible." | Action verb. Signature-phrase echo. |
| "Q3 Financial Highlights" | "Q3: three readouts, two filings." | Specific over abstract. Numbers earn the slide. |
| "Working Together for Patients" | "Patients first — every decision." | Passionate attribute. Declarative. Removes corporate filler. |
| "Future Outlook and Opportunities" | "What's next — and why it matters." | Drops corporate-speak ("outlook," "opportunities") for direct framing. |
| "Industry-Leading Science" | "Best-in-class. Three decades in." | Specific claim grounded in the heritage. Confident, not boastful. |
| "Key Achievements" | "What we shipped this quarter." | Plain language. "Shipped" reads driven. |

## Body copy

**❌ Off-brand:**
> Ionis is a leading biotechnology company that develops and commercializes innovative RNA-targeted medicines for patients with serious diseases. Through our pioneering work, we are committed to bringing life-changing therapies to those who need them most.

**✅ On-brand:**
> For three decades, Ionis has pioneered RNA-targeted medicines. We've turned impossible firsts into shipped medicines — and our pipeline is the deepest it's ever been.

Why: cuts "leading biotechnology company" filler. Uses "shipped" (driven verb) and "deepest it's ever been" (confident claim). Signature-phrase echo without copy-paste.

---

**❌ Off-brand:**
> Our team is excited to share recent progress on our investigational medicine for [indication]. Preliminary data suggests promising results, and we look forward to continued development in this important area.

**✅ On-brand:**
> Our investigational medicine for [indication] hit primary endpoint in Phase 2. Phase 3 starts next quarter.

Why: declarative facts, no hedge-language ("excited," "promising," "look forward to"). Passes the "could appear on any biotech's website" test — it can't, because it's specific.

---

**❌ Off-brand:**
> Patients are at the heart of everything we do, and we strive to make a meaningful difference in their lives through our cutting-edge science and unwavering commitment to excellence.

**✅ On-brand:**
> We push because patients can't wait. The science has to land — on time, in their hands.

Why: kills three biotech-trope phrases ("at the heart," "cutting-edge," "unwavering commitment"). Passionate attribute with a sense of urgency.

## Section openers

**❌ Off-brand opener:**
> Today I'll walk you through our pipeline progress, key milestones, and upcoming readouts. We're excited about the momentum we're seeing across our programs.

**✅ On-brand opener:**
> Three readouts. Two filings. One year. Here's where we are.

Why: numbers earn the opener. Removes "excited" / "momentum" filler. Driven attribute.

---

**❌ Off-brand opener:**
> In this section, we'll provide an overview of our scientific capabilities and how they differentiate us in the industry.

**✅ On-brand opener:**
> What makes Ionis different is older than the company.

Why: claims the heritage instead of teaching it. Sets up the RNA-targeted story without "we'll cover" scaffolding.

## Calls to action

| ❌ Off-brand | ✅ On-brand |
|---|---|
| "Learn more about our pipeline" | "See what's next" |
| "Contact us for partnership opportunities" | "Want to build something with us?" |
| "Thank you for your attention" | (Don't use a thank-you slide. Use a question or a signature phrase.) |
| "Any questions?" | "What know-how would you turn into a skill?" (or another specific question from the deck's argument) |

## What to avoid — patterns

These phrases and patterns flatten Ionis voice into generic biotech-speak. Watch for them in drafts and cut.

- "At the heart of…" / "Patients are at the heart of…"
- "Cutting-edge" / "world-class" / "industry-leading" (replaceable with specifics)
- "Excited to share / excited about / excited to announce"
- "We strive to…" / "We are committed to…" / "We work tirelessly to…"
- "Unwavering commitment" / "deep dedication" / "passionate about"
- "Solutions" as a generic noun ("our solutions help…")
- "Journey" as metaphor ("on our journey…")
- "Innovate" used as a verb without an object
- "Stakeholders" as an unqualified plural (be specific — patients, caregivers, providers)
- "Leverage" as a verb
- "Synergy" — never

These aren't on-brand; they aren't off-brand; they're the generic-pharma center of gravity. The Ionis voice sits outside that center on purpose.

## When the user pushes back on voice

If a user asks you to soften an on-brand headline because it "sounds aggressive" or "feels too confident":

1. Explain the brand voice mandate briefly (Confident is one of the five attributes; heritage = credibility).
2. Offer a more grounded variant that keeps the confidence but trims any swagger:
   - "Push beyond the impossible" → "Built to push beyond what's been possible"
   - "We pioneered RNA-targeted medicines" → "Pioneering work in RNA-targeted medicines is part of our story."
3. Let them choose. Don't argue past one round.

If they want to ship a hedged headline, ship it. Don't quietly re-confidence the rest of the deck to compensate.
